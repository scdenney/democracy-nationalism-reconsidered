# Literature Review Evidence Map — democracy, liberalism, and the content of nationhood

Compiled 2026-06-04 via four verified web-search strands + the second-stage empirical diagnostic.
DOIs verified by the search agents; items flagged `[confirm]` need a 30-second resolver check before submission.

## Review question
When, and for which components, are commitments to democracy and nationalism in tension in mass publics? Specifically: does endorsement of *liberal democratic rights* restrain *exclusionary* nationalism, and what conditions that relationship across regimes and political cultures?

## Gap verdict: **OPEN** (confirmation-plus-extension on two flanks; the core is unclaimed)
- The "civic ≠ automatically inclusive" point is partly established (Simonsen & Bonikowski 2020; Tamir 2019; Brubaker 1999/2017) → frame as extension.
- **Unclaimed:** (i) using *democratic-rights endorsement* (not identity content) as the restraining predictor; (ii) the **null-with-pride / negative-with-exclusion** decoupling; (iii) the **West ↔ East-Asian-democracy sign reversal**; (iv) that the moderator is the **civic/ethnic content of nationhood, not institutional liberalism (V-Dem)**; (v) separating principle from **performance** (Norris 1999) effects. Our note sits in that intersection.

## The hook (where the inherited claim comes from)
The premise "in the West, democratic values are inversely related to nationalism" is the setup the **Chinese popular-nationalism** literature states in order to contradict it for China:
- **Zhong, Yang, and Wonjae Hwang. 2020.** "Why Do Chinese Democrats Tend to Be More Nationalistic? Explaining Popular Nationalism in Urban China." *Journal of Contemporary China* 29(121): 61–74. doi:10.1080/10670564.2019.1621530. — contains the near-verbatim claim; finds Chinese liberal-democratic value holders are *more* nationalistic.
- **Liu, Wenchao, and Tangbiao Xiao. 2025/26.** "Three Puzzles of Chinese Popular Nationalism: Insights from Nationwide Longitudinal CSGS Data." *Asian Survey*. doi:10.1525/as.2025.2846273. — substantive democratic values linked to the *strongest* nationalism in China. (The note's original "Liu and Xiao" hook.)
- Our contribution: the reversal is **not** a China/authoritarian "regime-discontent" puzzle — it appears in consolidated East-Asian **democracies** (Taiwan, Korea), where it reflects democracy built *through* national mobilization.

## Evidence clusters

### Cluster 1 — The fault line is liberalism vs. exclusion, not democracy vs. nationalism (theory anchors)
- **Tamir, Yael. 2019.** "Not So Civic: Is There a Difference Between Ethnic and Civic Nationalism?" *Annual Review of Political Science* 22: 419–434. doi:10.1146/annurev-polisci-022018-024059. — operative cleavage is **liberal vs. illiberal**, not civic vs. ethnic. *Primary framing anchor.*
- **Mounk, Yascha. 2018.** *The People vs. Democracy.* Harvard UP. ISBN 9780674976825. — liberal democracy decomposing into illiberal democracy (rights-less) vs. undemocratic liberalism; tension = liberalism vs. majoritarian will.
- **Mudde, Cas. 2007.** *Populist Radical Right Parties in Europe.* Cambridge UP. doi:10.1017/CBO9780511492037. — **nativism** (nationalism × xenophobia) as core of the radical right = our "exclusion" construct.
- **Mudde, Cas, and Cristóbal Rovira Kaltwasser. 2017.** *Populism: A Very Short Introduction.* Oxford UP. ISBN 9780190234874. — populism as majoritarian/illiberal.
- **Greenfeld, Liah. 1992.** *Nationalism: Five Roads to Modernity.* Harvard UP. ISBN 9780674603196. — individualistic-civic vs. collectivistic-ethnic nationalism; civic↔liberal historically.
- **Tamir 1993** *Liberal Nationalism* (Princeton, ISBN 9780691001746); **Müller 2007** *Constitutional Patriotism* (Princeton, 9780691118598); **Habermas** (constitutional patriotism); **Miller 1995** *On Nationality* (OUP, 9780198280477); **Kymlicka 1995** *Multicultural Citizenship* (OUP, 9780198290919) — attachment routed through rights/principles need not be exclusionary.

### Cluster 2 — Democracy is a thick, contested concept; principles ≠ performance (measurement of the predictor)
- **Norris, Pippa (ed.). 1999.** *Critical Citizens.* Oxford UP. — distinguishes support for democratic **principles** from **performance** evaluations. *Anchors our two democracy predictors.*
- **Kirsch, Helen, and Christian Welzel. 2019.** "Democracy Misunderstood: Authoritarian Notions of Democracy around the Globe." *Social Forces* 98(1): 59–92. doi:10.1093/sf/soy114. — people load liberal vs. authoritarian content into "democracy"; liberalism is the hinge. *Closest conceptual anchor.*
- **Schedler, Andreas, and Rodolfo Sarsfield. 2007.** "Democrats with adjectives." *EJPR* 46(5): 637–659. doi:10.1111/j.1475-6765.2007.00708.x. — abstract democratic support hides illiberal democrats; measure liberal principles directly.
- **Ulbricht 2018** *CPS* 51(11):1387–1440 doi:10.1177/0010414018758751; **Cho 2014** *PRQ* 67(3):478–488 doi:10.1177/1065912914532721; **Cho 2015** *Political Studies* 63(1):240–258 doi:10.1111/1467-9248.12088 — democracy's meaning is contested/context-conditional.
- **Dalton 2008** *Political Studies* 56(1):76–98 doi:10.1111/j.1467-9248.2007.00718.x — duty vs. engaged citizenship norms (frames ISSP citizenship/participation items).
- **Chu, Yun-han, and Min-hua Huang. 2010.** "The Meanings of Democracy: Solving an Asian Puzzle." *Journal of Democracy* 21(4): 114–122. — East-Asian conceptions of democracy substantively distinctive.
- Caveat sources for autocracies: **Bratton, Mattes & Gyimah-Boadi 2005** (*Public Opinion, Democracy, and Market Reform in Africa*, Cambridge UP); **Schaffer 1998** (*Democracy in Translation*, Cornell UP).
- Defense vs. social-desirability objection: **Magalhães & Aarslew. 2025.** "Survey measures of democratic attitudes and social desirability bias." *PSRM* doi:10.1017/psrm.2025.10044 — no SD inflation of democratic-attitude items (Europe).

### Cluster 3 — Nationalism is multidimensional; pride ≠ exclusion (measurement of the outcome)
- **Kosterman, Rick, and Seymour Feshbach. 1989.** "Toward a Measure of Patriotic and Nationalistic Attitudes." *Political Psychology* 10(2): 257–274. doi:10.2307/3791647. — patriotism/pride vs. nationalism/superiority are distinct.
- **Schatz, Staub & Lavine. 1999.** "On the Varieties of National Attachment: Blind Versus Constructive Patriotism." *Political Psychology* 20(1): 151–174. doi:10.1111/0162-895X.00140.
- **Bonikowski, Bart, and Paul DiMaggio. 2016.** "Varieties of American Popular Nationalism." *American Sociological Review* 81(5): 949–980. doi:10.1177/0003122416663683. — *methodological template for disaggregating nationalism.*
- **Bonikowski 2016** *Annu. Rev. Sociol.* 42:427–449 doi:10.1146/annurev-soc-081715-074412 (note: 2016, not 2017); **Bonikowski 2017** *BJS* 68(S1):S181–S213 doi:10.1111/1468-4446.12325 — disaggregate nationalism from authoritarianism/populism.
- **Ariely 2012** *N&N* 18(3):461–482 doi:10.1111/j.1469-8129.2011.00532.x — pride/chauvinism/ethnic-membership behave differently cross-nationally.

### Cluster 4 — The content-of-nationhood → exclusion link, and its context-conditionality (closest empirical priors)
- **Pehrson, Samuel, Vivian L. Vignoles, and Rupert Brown. 2009.** "National Identification and Anti-Immigrant Prejudice: Individual and Contextual Effects of National Definitions." *Social Psychology Quarterly* 72(1): 24–38. doi:10.1177/019027250907200104. **(ISSP, 31 countries.)** — identification→prejudice link is conditional on whether nationhood is defined ethnically vs. civically, as a **country-level contextual effect**. *Closest prior work; the contextual-moderator template we adopt.*
- **Hjerm, Mikael. 1998.** "National Identities, National Pride and Xenophobia." *Acta Sociologica* 41(4): 335–347. doi:10.1177/000169939804100403. (ISSP 1995, 4 Western) — ethnic conception predicts xenophobia, pride does not.
- **Simonsen, Kristina Bakkær, and Bart Bonikowski. 2020.** "Is Civic Nationalism Necessarily Inclusive?" *EJPR* 59(1): 114–136. doi:10.1111/1475-6765.12337. — civic self-understanding still predicts anti-Muslim hostility (Europe).
- **Wright, Citrin & Wand. 2012.** *Political Psychology* 33(4):469–482 doi:10.1111/j.1467-9221.2012.00885.x; **Wright 2011** *Political Psychology* 32(5):837–862 doi:10.1111/j.1467-9221.2011.00843.x; **Citrin, Reingold & Green. 1990.** *Journal of Politics* 52(4):1124–1154 doi:10.2307/2131685 *(target-venue precedent).*
- **Shulman 2002** *CPS* 35(5):554–585 doi:10.1177/0010414002035005003; **Janmaat 2006** *ERS* 29(1):50–78 doi:10.1080/01419870500352363 `[confirm]` — civic/ethnic/cultural typology; West/East mapping unreliable.

### Cluster 5 — The Western/cross-national mainstream we reverse (the foil)
- **Gabrielsson, Daniel, Mikael Hjerm, and Maureen A. ### Cluster 6 — Measurement invariance of ISSP scales (defends the design)
- **Davidov, Eldad. 2009.** "Measurement Equivalence of Nationalism and Constructive Patriotism in the ISSP: 34 Countries." *Political Analysis* 17(1): 64–82. doi:10.1093/pan/mpn014. — **metric invariance holds; full scalar fails → compare associations across countries, NOT latent means.** *Direct license for our correlational, within-context design.*
- **Reeskens, Tim, and Marc Hooghe. 2010.** "Beyond the Civic–Ethnic Dichotomy: ... Across Thirty-Three Countries." *Nations and Nationalism* 16(4): 579–597. doi:10.1111/j.1469-8129.2010.00446.x. — citizenship-criteria battery not cross-nationally equivalent; comparison "difficult."
- **Larsen, Christian Albrekt. 2017.** "Revitalizing the 'civic' and 'ethnic' distinction." *Nations and Nationalism* 23(4): 970–993. doi:10.1111/nana.12345. — strength vs. civic-ethnic balance are separable dimensions.
- Alignment/approximate-invariance recommendation: **Davidov, Muthén & Schmidt. 2018.** *Sociological Methods & Research* editorial doi:10.1177/0049124118789708; **Asparouhov & Muthén 2014** alignment.
- East-Asia mechanism: **Shin, Gi-Wook. 2006.** *Ethnic Nationalism in Korea.* Stanford UP. doi:10.1515/9780804768016. **Chu 2004** *Asian Survey* 44(4):484–512 doi:10.1525/as.2004.44.4.484.

## Contribution diagnosis → central claim (empirically confirmed by the second-stage diagnostic)
**Whether commitment to democratic rights restrains exclusionary nationalism depends on the civic-vs-ethnic *content* of nationhood, not on how liberal-democratic a regime's institutions are.** In civic-national settings (the West) democratic-rights endorsement is negatively related to exclusion and ~null with pride; in ethnically-imagined democracies (Taiwan, Korea) — institutionally as liberal as the West (V-Dem) — the relationship vanishes or reverses. Country-level: civic-ethnic content predicts the slope (β≈−0.11, p<0.001, adjR²≈0.47) while V-Dem liberal/electoral components add **nothing** net (p=0.96 / 0.62). Democratic *performance* evaluations travel with national pride across regimes — a separate axis. "Democracy vs. nationalism" is the wrong frame: it is liberalism (not democracy) vs. exclusion (not nationalism), and only where nationhood is civic.
